Governance Principles

Constitutional Charter of Agility Game Governance

⸻

Executive Summary

The Governance Principles establish the constitutional boundaries of Agility Game Governance.

If:

Governance Engine

defines:

How Governance Operates

then Governance Principles define:

What Governance Must Never Violate

These principles represent the highest authority within the Governance Repository.

Every:

* World
* Mission
* Agent
* Department
* Product
* Service
* Architecture
* Organization

must comply with these principles.

No governance capability is exempt.

No governance capability supersedes them.

⸻

Purpose

The Governance Principles ensure governance remains:

* trustworthy
* transparent
* explainable
* scalable
* evidence-driven

while allowing continuous innovation.

The principles provide stability without creating rigidity.

Principle 1 — Governability

Statement

Everything within Agility Game must be governable.

⸻

Purpose

Governability is the foundation of trust.

Objects that cannot be governed:

Cannot Be Trusted.

Objects that cannot be trusted:

Cannot Be Promoted.

⸻

Requirements

Every governable object must possess:

Identity
Purpose
Owner
Standards
Validators
Evidence
Assurance

⸻

Constitutional Rule

Governability precedes promotion.

⸻

Principle 3 — Determinism

Statement

Deterministic verification is preferred over probabilistic evaluation.

⸻

Purpose

Trust requires repeatability.

Repeatability requires determinism.

⸻

Deterministic Outcome
```
Input A
      ↓
Result X
```
Always.

⸻

Constitutional Rule

AI may assist governance.

AI should not replace deterministic validation when deterministic validation is practical.

⸻

⸻

Purpose
